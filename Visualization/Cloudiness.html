Latitude vs. Cloudiness

The chart shows scattered data when it comes to cloudiness vs latitude. That could be due to the temperature that occurred the day
the data was collected. Since heat rises, and the hot air mixes with cold air, clouds would form from the mix. Being at the equator should provide
data that shows high cloudiness because the sun is closer to the equator. Yet, the data is consistent with the spread of cloudiness at and from the center.
